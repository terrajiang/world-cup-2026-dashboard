Group Stage

  • 48 teams are split into 12 groups of 4.
  • Each team plays the other 3 teams in its group once.
  • A win is 3 points, a draw is 1 point, and a loss is 0 points.
  • The top 2 teams in every group advance.
  • The 8 best third-place teams also advance.

Tiebreakers

  • Teams are ranked first by points.
  • If tied, goal difference is checked next.
  • If still tied, goals scored is checked next.
  • If teams are still level, head-to-head and fair-play rules can decide it.

Knockout Stage

  • 32 teams enter a single-elimination bracket.
  • If a knockout match is tied after 90 minutes, extra time is played.
  • If it is still tied after extra time, penalties decide the winner.
  • Winners move on; losers are out, except semifinal losers play the third-place game.
More detailed rules

Group Ranking Order

  1. Total points from all group matches.
  2. Goal difference from all group matches.
  3. Goals scored in all group matches.
  4. Points in matches between the tied teams.
  5. Goal difference in matches between the tied teams.
  6. Goals scored in matches between the tied teams.
  7. Fair-play points, where cards can hurt a team's ranking.
  8. Drawing of lots if teams still cannot be separated.

Best Third-Place Teams

The 12 third-place teams are compared in one table. The best 8 advance to the Round of 32. They are compared by points, then goal difference, then goals scored, then fair-play rules if needed.

Knockout Matches

Knockout games cannot end in a draw. If the score is tied after 90 minutes, teams play two 15-minute halves of extra time. If still tied, a penalty shootout decides who advances.

Seeding & Draw

The 48 teams were split into four pots of 12. Pot 1 included the three host nations plus the nine highest-ranked qualified teams. Pots 2, 3, and 4 used the FIFA ranking order, with playoff placeholders placed in Pot 4.

Each group got one team from each pot. Mexico, Canada, and the United States were pre-placed for host scheduling. Groups generally avoid teams from the same confederation, except UEFA groups can have up to two European teams.
